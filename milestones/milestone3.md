## Reflection

### 1. What went well during this unit?

 - Setting up GitHub project‑management tools (Issues, Milestones, and Project boards) helped bring more structure to our workflow.
 - Team members had clearly assigned tasks, which provided a general sense of direction.
 - Troubleshooting and communication between the members who were actively engaged were productive and helped move the project forward.

### 2. What were some challenges your group faced in this unit?

 - Communication decreased significantly this week, which made it harder to coordinate development in a way that supported each other’s tasks and dependencies.

 - Several of our GitHub Issues were written too broadly, which created gray areas about what each person was responsible for. When trying to fill in those gaps, some work unintentionally overlapped with another member’s tasks or conflicted with their branch.

 - Because we were all working on separate branches without enough check‑ins, it was difficult to know whether updates were being integrated in the right order or whether someone else was already building a related feature.

### 4. Which features and user stories would you consider “at risk”? How will you change your plan if those items remain “at risk”?

The features most “at risk” for our team are the social‑focused components—such as the post feed, comments, direct messages, and global hashtags—because they rely on shared structures and coordinated development.

Some of our GitHub Issues were written broadly, which created gray areas about ownership and led to unintentional overlap when filling in missing details. If these items remain at risk, we can shift our focus toward the core project‑based functionality, prioritizing project creation, display, and browsing while reducing the emphasis on social‑media features.

We can also simplify interactions by limiting them to basic connections and narrowing our user stories to logged‑in users only, since viewers without accounts cannot access content anyway.

### 5. What additional support will you need in upcoming units as you continue to work on your final project?

 - Meeting at least 1–2 times per week via video call would help ensure alignment, reduce duplicated work, and keep everyone on the same page.

 - We need a shared conversation to clarify the overall vision of the final project, since different interpretations are emerging as development continues.

 - More detailed, specific GitHub Issues would help prevent overlap and make it clearer what each person is responsible for.

 - Additional review and guidance from Tech Fellows would help us stay on track and ensure we’re implementing features in a way that supports the project’s goals.
